Overview

During my internship at Trane Technologies, I supported the engineering and production of large, custom HVAC units built to meet specific customer, site, and performance requirements. These systems were engineered-to-order and produced at scale, requiring close coordination between design, manufacturing, and operations.

My work focused on bridging design intent and shop-floor execution. Rather than working on a single product, I contributed across many units and workstreams, supporting over 100 completed builds. Customers ranged from industrial and commercial facilities to government, healthcare, and high-reliability applications, where accuracy, documentation quality, and manufacturability were critical.

The role emphasized practical engineering: maintaining and updating CAD models, improving drawings used directly in production, and resolving design and assembly issues during manufacturing. Each task required balancing customer requirements with real-world fabrication and assembly constraints.

System / Architecture

The systems supported during this internship consisted of large, modular HVAC assemblies composed of structural frames, airflow components, mechanical systems, and electrical integrations. Each unit followed a common architectural framework while being customized to meet specific customer requirements.

CAD models and drawings served as the central source of truth, linking engineering intent to fabrication and assembly. Changes to one subsystem often affected others, requiring careful coordination to maintain alignment across components and build stages.

The overall approach prioritized modularity, standard interfaces, and clear documentation so that variation could be managed without disrupting production flow or quality.
